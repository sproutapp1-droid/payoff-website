## Generic advice is everywhere. Personalised advice is rare.

Search "how to pay off debt" and you'll find thousands of articles telling you to "make a budget," "cut unnecessary expenses," and "pay more than the minimum." That advice isn't wrong. But it's about as useful as telling someone who wants to run a marathon to "run more."

The problem with generic debt advice is that it doesn't account for **your specific situation**. Your debts have different balances, different interest rates, different minimum payments, and different due dates. You have a specific income, specific expenses, and a specific amount you can put toward debt each month. Cookie-cutter tips can't factor in any of that.

An **AI debt coach** changes this completely. By analysing your actual financial data, it gives advice that's calibrated to your exact situation, right now, today.

## What makes an AI debt coach different from ChatGPT?

You might be wondering: "Can't I just ask ChatGPT for debt advice?" You can. But there's a fundamental difference between a general AI chatbot and a purpose-built AI debt coach.

### A general chatbot knows nothing about you

When you ask ChatGPT "should I pay off my credit card or my car loan first?", it gives you a general framework. It might explain the snowball and avalanche methods. But it can't tell you **which one saves you more money with your specific debts**, because it doesn't know your balances, rates, or payment amounts.

### An AI debt coach sees your real data

When Payoff's AI coach answers the same question, it already knows:

- Every debt you've entered (balance, APR, minimum payment, due date)
- Your chosen payoff strategy (snowball, avalanche, or hybrid)
- Your monthly disposable income and extra payment amount
- Your payment history and progress so far
- Your savings goals and timeline
- Your stress level and motivation (from mood check-ins)

This context transforms every response from generic to specific.

Payoff's AI coach uses OpenAI's GPT-4o-mini model, called through a secure backend. Your data never leaves the secure server environment, and the AI has no memory between sessions beyond your last 5 messages. It sees your debt data only when you're actively chatting.
</Callout>

## Real example conversations

Here's what the difference looks like in practice. These examples show the kind of responses Payoff's AI coach provides when it has access to your actual debt data.

### Example 1: "Where should I put my tax refund?"

**You:** I just got a $600 tax refund. Where should I put it?

> **AI Coach:** Great news on the refund! Looking at your debts, here's what I'd suggest:
>
> Your Visa card ($3,200 at 22.9% APR) is costing you about $61/month in interest alone. If you put the full $600 there, you'd:
> - Reduce the balance to $2,600
> - Save approximately $340 in interest over the remaining payoff period
> - Shave about 2 months off your Visa payoff date
>
> Your car loan ($8,400 at 5.9%) is much cheaper to carry. Putting $600 there would only save about $85 in interest.
>
> My recommendation: put all $600 toward the Visa. The interest rate difference makes it nearly 4x more impactful there.

Compare that to generic advice, which would say something like "consider putting extra money toward your highest-interest debt." The AI coach did the actual calculation and showed you exactly how much you'd save.

### Example 2: "I'm feeling overwhelmed"

**You:** I just looked at my total debt and I feel sick. How am I ever going to pay off $28,000?

> **AI Coach:** I hear you, and that feeling is completely normal. Looking at $28,000 as one number is overwhelming. But let's reframe it.
>
> Based on your current plan, you're paying off $485/month above minimums. At this pace, here's what your timeline actually looks like:
> - Your store card ($900) will be gone in just 2 months
> - Your medical bill ($2,100) will be gone by month 7
> - That means by summer, you'll have eliminated 2 debts and freed up $130/month in minimums
>
> You don't need to think about $28,000. You just need to think about $900 right now. That's your only job for the next 8 weeks. Can you do $900? I think you can.

This response is both emotionally supportive and data-driven. It acknowledges the feeling, then uses the person's real numbers to make the situation feel manageable.

### Example 3: "Should I change my strategy?"

**You:** I've been using the snowball method but I'm wondering if avalanche would be better for me.

> **AI Coach:** Good question. Let me run both scenarios with your current debts.
>
> **Snowball (your current plan):**
> - Debt-free date: March 2028
> - Total interest paid: $4,720
> - Next payoff: Store card in 2 months
>
> **Avalanche:**
> - Debt-free date: January 2028
> - Total interest paid: $3,890
> - Next payoff: Credit card in 5 months
>
> Switching to avalanche would save you $830 in interest and get you debt-free 2 months sooner. But you wouldn't see your first debt eliminated for another 5 months instead of 2.
>
> Since you've already paid off one debt (congrats!), you've proven you can stick with a plan. I think you'd do well with avalanche now. But if those quick wins are keeping you going, staying on snowball is perfectly valid too. What feels right?

The coach doesn't just answer the question. It runs both calculations, presents the trade-offs in concrete terms, and then respects the person's autonomy to decide.

## Privacy and security

Financial data is sensitive, and you should understand exactly how the AI coach handles yours.

### What the AI can see

When you send a message, the coach receives:
- Your debt list (balances, rates, minimums, due dates)
- Your payoff strategy and extra payment amount
- Your last 5 chat messages for conversation context
- Your savings goals (if any)

### What the AI cannot see

- Your bank account numbers or login credentials (Payoff never asks for these)
- Your full name or personal identification
- Other users' data
- Your chat history beyond the last 5 messages

### How data flows

1. You type a message in the app
2. The app sends your message plus your debt context to a **secure Supabase Edge Function** (server-side)
3. The Edge Function calls OpenAI's API with your data
4. The response comes back through the same secure channel
5. Your OpenAI API key is stored on the server and never exposed to the app

<Callout type="warning">
Your data is never used to train AI models. OpenAI's API terms explicitly state that data sent through the API is not used for model training. Your financial information stays between you and your Payoff account.
</Callout>

### No bank access required

Unlike some financial apps that ask you to connect your bank accounts, Payoff works entirely on data you manually enter. This means:
- No third-party access to your bank
- No risk of credential exposure
- You control exactly what data the app has

## Frequently asked questions

### Is the AI coach a replacement for a financial advisor?

No. The AI coach is excellent for debt payoff strategy, budgeting guidance, and motivation. For complex situations like tax planning, bankruptcy decisions, investment strategy, or legal matters, consult a certified financial professional.

### Can the AI coach access my bank account?

No. Payoff never asks for bank credentials. All debt information is manually entered by you. The AI coach only sees the data you've added to the app.

### How accurate are the AI's calculations?

The coach's calculations are based on standard amortisation formulas using your exact balances, rates, and payment amounts. They're as accurate as the data you provide. If your balances or rates change, update them in the app for the most precise guidance.

### What if the AI gives bad advice?

The AI coach is designed to provide conservative, well-established financial guidance. It won't recommend risky strategies or speculative investments. However, it's a tool, not a fiduciary. Always use your own judgment, and consult a professional for major financial decisions.
